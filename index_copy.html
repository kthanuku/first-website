Fray and Mend

Threads unravel, loose and thin,
Pulled by time, by wear, by sin.
Edges frayed where hands once held,
The fabric worn, the seams unspelled.

A tear begins with silent strain,
The quiet tug of grief or rain.
Winds will pull and tempests bite,
Until what’s whole slips out of sight.

Yet still, beneath the broken weave,
The loom remembers how to breathe.
A needle hums, a steady song,
To stitch back all that time made wrong.

Hands rethread what once was lost,
Soft with care, though sharp the cost.
Knot by knot, the seams confess
A whispered tale of tenderness.

For things made new still hold the scars,
Lines of gold where wounds once marred.
Fray and mend, and fray again,
Is how we love, is how we mend.

The fabric stronger, though not the same—
From tears, we find a different name.
So let it fray, and let it mend,
For life begins where things can end.

Whispers of the Wane

The moon fades, a thinning whisper in the sky,
Waning like thought slipping away
Before you can catch it.

It hangs, barely holding on,
Its light softer,
As if it’s giving in to night.

The weight—a quiet farewell,
But a pulse, a moment between breaths,
Where even the stars seem to wait
For the next cycle to begin,
Making way for the darkness to bloom.

The Space Between

Home is the scent of earth after rain,
A scent that carries me through winds of change.
The hyphen stretches—a thread worn thin,
A space between, where all begins.

Caught between clocks, I lose my pace,
Time flows differently in this in-between space.
Here, I am foreign; there, I’m apart,
Suspended in silence, neither an end or start.

This space is tension, a thread pulled tight,
A bridge suspended between day and night.
The scent of home stirs, sharp and sweet,
A compass that falters but still guides my feet.

Yet in this pause, I start to see
The hyphen as more than a divide for me.
It holds its own, a bridge unbound,
A place where roots and wings are found.

I carry it forward, though it pulls me back,
A thread through time, on an endless track.
Caught between clocks, I am both and none—
A fleeting shadow, a rising sun.

The hyphen breathes, it lets me be,
A space between that shapes all of me.
It aches, it stirs, but it will suffice—
This tireless balance, this tireless strife.
